California is 770 miles long. It produces more agricultural output than most countries. It is home to the largest Persian, Chinese, Vietnamese, Korean, and Mexican diaspora communities in the US. And it has, as a result, the most diverse and excellent ice cream culture in the world.

The California Ice Cream Trail is 27 stops spread across this geography — from the Mexican-inspired sorbets of San Diego to the Sonoma wine country creameries, from Hollywood's Persian bastani to the organic soft serve at a dairy farm overlooking Tomales Bay.

This is how to plan your trip.

## The Three Routes

### Route 1: The Coastal NorCal Run (3–4 days, San Francisco base)

**Day 1: San Francisco (4 stops)**
Start at [Bi-Rite Creamery](/shops/bi-rite-creamery) when it opens at 11am. Walk to Dolores Park. Walk to [Humphry Slocombe](/shops/humphry-slocombe). Rest. Afternoon at [Smitten Ice Cream](/shops/smitten-ice-cream) in Hayes Valley. Evening at [Salt & Straw](/shops/salt-and-straw) on Fillmore.

*Total damage: 4 scoops. Recommended order: single, wait 30 minutes, repeat.*

**Day 2: East Bay (3 stops)**
[Tucker's Ice Cream](/shops/tuckers-ice-cream) in Alameda opens at 11am — take the tube from Oakland. Then [Fentons Creamery](/shops/fentons-creamery) in the Oakland hills for the Black & Tan. Late afternoon: [Loard's](/shops/lords-ice-cream) in Pleasanton if you're heading south.

**Day 3: North Bay and Wine Country**
Drive Highway 1 north from San Francisco. Stop at [Straus Family Creamery](/shops/straus-family-creamery) in Marshall for the on-farm soft serve (call ahead to confirm hours). Continue to Sebastopol for [Screamin' Mimi's](/shops/screamin-mimis). Optional: [Three Twins](/shops/three-twins-ice-cream) in San Rafael on the return.

**Day 4: South Bay**
[Naia Gelato](/shops/naia-gelato) in Los Gatos. [Penny Ice Creamery](/shops/penny-ice-creamery) in Santa Cruz. [Marianne's Ice Cream](/shops/marianne-ice-cream) two blocks away. This is an ice cream density situation — Santa Cruz can handle three shops in an afternoon.

### Route 2: The LA Basin Loop (2–3 days)

**Day 1: Hollywood, Eastside, SGV**
[Mashti Malone's](/shops/mashti-malones) in Hollywood at lunch. [Scoops](/shops/scoops) in East Hollywood (check their hours — they sometimes close early). [Carmela Ice Cream](/shops/carmela-ice-cream) in Pasadena for afternoon. [Fosselman's](/shops/fosselmans) in Alhambra before dinner.

**Day 2: Westside**
[Sweet Rose Creamery](/shops/sweet-rose-creamery) at the Brentwood Country Mart. [Coolhaus](/shops/coolhaus) in Culver City. Optional: drive to Santa Monica pier area and get a scoop from any of the pop-ups that set up there in summer.

**Day 3: South and East**
[Hammond's Gourmet Ice Cream](/shops/hammonds-gourmet) in San Diego (2 hours south). [Mariposa Ice Cream](/shops/mariposa-ice-cream) in North Park. [Gelato Paradiso](/shops/gelato-paradiso) in Palm Springs (2 hours east from San Diego).

*Palm Springs makes the most sense as a standalone overnight — arrive in the evening, eat gelato, wake up in the desert, drive back to LA through the San Bernardino Mountains.*

---

### Route 3: The Central Valley & Sierra (1–2 days)

**Day 1: Sacramento to Tahoe**
[Big Dipper Creamery](/shops/big-dipper-creamery) in Sacramento (midtown) in the morning. Drive east on I-80 into the Sierra. Arrive [Tahoe Blue Cow](/shops/tahoe-blue-cow) in Tahoe City in the afternoon. Eat your brown butter cookie dough scoop with a view of Lake Tahoe. Stay in Tahoe.

**Day 2: Return via Yosemite corridor**
The longer return through the mountains passes through gold rush country and the Northern San Joaquin Valley. This area doesn't have Trail stops yet, but it's worth knowing for future Trail expansion.

## Tips for the Full Trail

### Pacing Yourself

One scoop per stop, shared if possible. Ice cream fatigue is real — after four stops, your palate stops registering what it's tasting. The recommended approach: one stop per 2–3 hours, palate-cleansing snacks (savory, salty) between stops.

### Seasonal Considerations

**Summer (June–September):** Best stone fruit flavors, longest hours, most crowded. Arrive at opening.

**Fall (September–November):** Second best. The NorCal fog retreats ("second summer" in SF), Sonoma apple and harvest flavors appear, lines are shorter.

**Winter (December–February):** Some shops close early or reduce hours, especially the farm locations. Bay Area shops stay open year-round. Call ahead for Santa Cruz, Sebastopol, and Marshall.

**Spring (March–May):** Strawberry season begins March in Watsonville (supplying most Bay Area shops). First fresh fruit flavors after winter. Excellent time to visit.

## The Philosophy of the Trail

The California Ice Cream Trail is a document of what California actually is: a place of extraordinary agricultural diversity (the best dairy, fruit, nuts, and honey in the country), layered with immigration waves that brought entirely different dessert traditions, served in an environment where the weather almost always cooperates.

The Trail spans a century of history — Fentons in 1894, Fosselman's in 1919, McConnell's in 1949 — and the present moment: Smitten making ice cream with liquid nitrogen, Salt & Straw releasing a Thanksgiving turkey flavor, Humphry Slocombe's daily whiteboard of experiments.

You don't need to eat all 27 to understand what the Trail is showing you. But if you do — across multiple trips, over the seasons — California becomes something you understand differently than you did before. A place that expresses its identity not in monuments or museums but in the food it produces and shares.

That seems about right.
