Los Angeles is the most diverse ice cream city in California and arguably the most interesting in the country. The city's demographics — the largest Persian community outside Iran, major Korean and Chinese populations, a vast Mexican-American community, and the Westside food elite — mean that ice cream here reflects genuinely different traditions, not just different flavors.

Here's how to eat your way through it.

## Hollywood: The Persian Quarter

### Mashti Malone's

1525 North La Brea in Hollywood is the address for the best Persian ice cream (bastani) outside of Iran. The **rosewater saffron pistachio** is the classic order: Iranian saffron gives it a golden hue, rose water provides a floral complexity unlike anything in the Western tradition, and crushed pistachios add texture.

The shop is open until midnight every night, which makes it the ideal destination after a Hollywood Bowl concert, a show at the Fonda, or a movie at the Arclight's replacement theaters.

Mashti Malone's is the kind of place that transforms how you think about ice cream. After the bastani, everything else tastes a bit one-dimensional.

### What to Order

Beyond the rosewater saffron, try the **saffron raisin** (more traditional, less sweet), the **akbar mashti** (the original bastani with cream nuggets folded in), and ask about any seasonal specials. Don't skip the saffron even if you're unfamiliar — it's the experience.

## East Hollywood: The Underground Scene

### Scoops

Tai Kim's shop on Heliotrope Drive has no website worth speaking of and has been making the best weird ice cream in LA since 2004. The menu is written on a whiteboard. It changes daily based on what Tai feels like making.

The **brown bread** is the cult classic — molasses-dark base with toasted breadcrumbs. But any given day might feature **avocado jalapeño**, **bourbon blackberry**, **activated charcoal vanilla** (before that was a trend), or flavors that don't have obvious category names.

This is ice cream as expression of individual creativity. There is nothing quite like it in Southern California.

**Pro tip:** Cash is preferred, and the shop closes early when flavors run out. Arrive by 3pm.

## The Westside: Farm-to-Scoop

### Sweet Rose Creamery (Santa Monica)

Josh Loeb and Zoe Nathan built their small restaurant empire on farmers market sourcing, and Sweet Rose Creamery is the dessert expression of that philosophy. At the Brentwood Country Mart location (the flagship), the menu changes weekly based on Santa Monica Farmers Market availability.

The **caramelized banana** is the year-round anchor — deeply transformed fruit in a rich cream base. In summer, the stone fruit specials (caramelized peach, nectarine verbena) are worth checking their Instagram for before making the drive.

### Coolhaus (Culver City)

Natasha Case built Coolhaus starting from a rented truck at Coachella in 2009 and scaled it into a national brand without losing the craft quality. The **dirty mint chip sandwich** — fresh mint ice cream (grassy-green, not candy-bright) between two thick double chocolate cookies — is the signature item.

The Culver City brick-and-mortar is the original storefront location. The architecture puns on the menu names are either charming or eye-roll-inducing depending on your tolerance.

## The San Gabriel Valley: The Real Find

Most food guides miss the San Gabriel Valley because it's outside the LA basin's trendy zip codes. This is their loss and your opportunity.

### Carmela Ice Cream (Pasadena)

The **Earl Grey tea with shortbread** is the calling card at Carmela on Washington Boulevard. Bergamot oil from the tea provides a perfumed complexity; the shortbread pieces give sweetness and crunch. It's the most sophisticated flavor on the Westside-to-SGV spectrum.

### Fosselman's (Alhambra)

Fosselman's has been in Alhambra since 1919. The **fresh mango sorbet** (seasonal) is the reason most food writers bring up this shop, and rightly so — Ataulfo mangoes at peak ripeness, pureed and churned, with nothing added. But the full menu is a document of 100 years of immigrant Los Angeles: taro, red bean, lychee, ube, alongside the traditional American standards.

## South LA and Beyond: Thrifty Ice Cream

No LA ice cream guide is complete without **Thrifty ice cream at Rite Aid**. The **chocolate malted krunch**, sold from a cylindrical scoop machine, has been a Los Angeles institution since 1940. The price is legendarily low. The ice cream is legitimately good. And the experience of eating it in a pharmacy parking lot while watching the LA neighborhood around you is, in a specific and unironic way, one of the city's great pleasures.

## Planning Your LA Ice Cream Day

**Hollywood + Eastside (half day):**
- Mashti Malone's (La Brea/Hollywood, open from noon)
- Drive east to Scoops (East Hollywood, 15 minutes)
- Optional extension: Carmela in Pasadena (30 minutes)

**Westside Full Day:**
- Sweet Rose Creamery (Santa Monica/Brentwood)
- Coolhaus (Culver City)
- Evening: Drive to Hollywood for Mashti Malone's midnight scoop

**San Gabriel Valley Deep Dive:**
- Fosselman's (Alhambra, for lunch + ice cream)
- Carmela (Pasadena)
- Huntington Library grounds walk (free with Pasadena Public Library card some days)

## Getting Around

LA is a car city and ice cream is no exception. The distances between these shops require driving. The good news: most have parking, and the drives are short enough to combine 2–3 shops in a few hours.

The Metro is only useful for the Hollywood and Pasadena stops.
