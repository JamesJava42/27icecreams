## 130 Years of Scooping

Gus Fenton opened this creamery on Piedmont Avenue in 1894, making it one of the oldest ice cream destinations in the American West. The original recipes — made with fresh dairy from local farms — have been preserved through multiple ownership changes and a major fire that destroyed and rebuilt the location.

## The Pixar Connection

When Pete Docter and the Pixar team were developing *Up*, they chose Fentons as the ice cream shop that elderly Carl promises young Ellie they'll visit together. The film brought a surge of tourists from around the world, all arriving to sit in the same booths and order the same Black & Tan sundae that Carl and Ellie never got to share.

## The Black & Tan

The sundae itself is deceptively simple: a scoop of rich vanilla, a scoop of deep chocolate, house-made hot fudge, whipped cream, and a cherry. The genius is in the components — Fentons makes their own hot fudge and their own ice cream, and both are exceptional on their own merits.

## The Full Experience

Fentons isn't just an ice cream stop — it's a full diner with savory food. Come for lunch, browse the vintage memorabilia covering every wall, and understand why this place has lasted 130 years.
