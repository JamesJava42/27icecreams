## A Century in Alhambra

Bill Fosselman opened this shop in 1919, and the family has run it continuously through four generations. Unlike many century-old businesses that have been sold and rebranded, Fosselman's is still owned by the Fosselman family, still in the original Alhambra location, and still making ice cream in the same tradition.

## The San Gabriel Valley Context

Alhambra sits in the heart of the San Gabriel Valley, a region with the highest concentration of Chinese, Vietnamese, and Filipino immigrants in the continental US, alongside a substantial Mexican-American population. Fosselman's serves all of them with a menu that ranges from traditional American flavors to lychee, taro, red bean, and the fresh mango sorbet.

## The Mango Sorbet

Ataulfo mangoes — small, golden, fiberless, intensely sweet — are the gold standard for fresh mango preparations. Fosselman's sources them during peak season (May through August) and churns a sorbet that contains essentially nothing but fruit. No filler, no fake mango flavoring. Just the concentrated result of extraordinary fruit.

## The Full Menu

Fosselman's offers over 50 flavors including flavors you won't find anywhere else on the Trail: lychee, durian (for the brave), avocado, and ube (purple yam). For anyone exploring the full diversity of California's food culture, this is a mandatory stop.
