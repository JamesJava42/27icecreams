## Liquid Nitrogen in Orange County

Creamistry founder Arthur Yin started with the liquid nitrogen format (same science as Smitten in San Francisco, different cultural focus) and built his menu to reflect the food preferences of Orange County's large Asian-American community.

## Why Thai Tea Ice Cream

Thai iced tea (cha yen) is one of the most loved drinks in Thai cuisine and has become enormously popular across Asian-American restaurant culture in the US. The combination of strong black tea, sweetened condensed milk, and star anise creates a distinctive orange-red color and a warm, creamy flavor.

As ice cream, the tea's tannins become structural while the condensed milk's sweetness anchors the flavor. The star anise note comes through subtly in the finish. The color is a deep burnt orange — visually striking in a cup or cone.

## The Orange County Food Scene

Irvine and surrounding Orange County cities have produced some of Southern California's most interesting food businesses, particularly in the Asian-American food space. Creamistry's roots here are appropriate — it serves a community that has historically been underrepresented in food media.

## Nationally Franchised

Creamistry has franchised across the US with dozens of locations. It's the most scalable concept on the California Trail — you may have one near you already.
