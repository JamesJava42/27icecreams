## The Story Behind the Scoop

When Anne Walker and Kris Hoogerhyde opened Bi-Rite Creamery on 18th Street in the Mission in 2006, they had one goal: make ice cream that tasted like it came from a backyard party, not a factory. Their salted caramel became the flavor that defined a generation of Bay Area dessert culture.

The recipe uses Straus Family Creamery organic cream — sourced from a 30-minute drive north in Marshall — and a house-made caramel cooked low and slow before fleur de sel from France is folded in at the last second. The result is a deeply complex flavor that hits sweet, smoky, and briny all at once.

## Why the Line Is Worth It

Bi-Rite regularly sells out of salted caramel by mid-afternoon on weekends. The shop opens at 11am; serious fans arrive by 10:45. The creamery is directly across from Dolores Park, and eating a scoop on the grass watching the city is one of San Francisco's great simple pleasures.

## What to Get Alongside It

Pair with their brown sugar soft serve or a scoop of ricanelas (cinnamon ice cream with snickerdoodle cookies). The honey lavender is a close second in the cult-classic rankings.

## Getting There

Take BART to 16th Street Mission and walk four blocks east. Street parking exists but prepare for the Mission's famously competitive scene. Most visitors arrive on foot or by Muni.
