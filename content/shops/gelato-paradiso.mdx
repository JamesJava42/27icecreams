## Why Bronte Pistachios Are Different

The Sicilian town of Bronte sits on the lava fields of Mount Etna. The volcanic soil — rich in minerals and dramatically different in chemistry from any other pistachio-growing region — produces a pistachio with a distinct flavor profile: greener, more herbal, with a natural sweetness that comes without added sugar.

Gelato Paradiso sources directly from Bronte producers. The paste arrives in Italy-packed containers and gets turned into gelato within days of arrival.

## The Gelato Difference

Gelato is distinct from American ice cream in two critical ways: lower fat content (more milk, less cream) and less air churned in (denser). The lower overrun means you taste more flavor per bite. The lower fat means flavors are cleaner and less coated on the palate.

For pistachio especially, gelato is the correct medium. The nuttiness comes through with a clarity that high-fat ice cream would mute.
