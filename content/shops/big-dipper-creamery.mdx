## Sacramento's Farm-to-Scoop Pioneer

Sacramento sits in the heart of the Sacramento Valley, California's most productive agricultural region — the source of almonds, walnuts, tomatoes, stone fruits, rice, and dozens of other crops. Big Dipper Creamery built its entire identity around sourcing from this abundance.

## The Honey Bourbon Pecan

Three local ingredients: wildflower honey from a local apiary within ten miles of the shop, California whiskey from a small-batch Sacramento Valley distillery, and pecans from Winters (a small farming town in Yolo County known for its walnuts and pecans).

The bourbon is not added for novelty — it serves as an aromatic bridge between the honey's sweetness and the pecans' earth. The alcohol level is controlled so you taste the oak and grain complexity without intoxication.

## The Central Valley Food Renaissance

Sacramento has been building a serious restaurant scene for the past decade, and Big Dipper is part of this. The Tower District and Midtown neighborhoods have become destinations for food that rivals Bay Area quality at lower prices.

## Seasonal Availability

The honey bourbon pecan is a year-round staple. Seasonal specials include Blenheim apricot (June–August), Gravenstein apple crumble (September–October), and Meyer lemon (winter). All feature sourcing from named farms within driving distance.
