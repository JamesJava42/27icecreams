## Los Gatos' Hidden Gem

Los Gatos sits at the edge of the Santa Cruz Mountains in Santa Clara County — wealthy, tree-lined, with a downtown that has resisted chain retail better than most Silicon Valley towns. Naia opened here in 2012 and built a reputation primarily through word of mouth and local food press.

## Why Avocado Works as Gelato

The skeptic's objection is reasonable: avocado is savory, fatty, and mild. The answer is that all three of those qualities are ice cream assets. Savory provides complexity. Fat creates body and richness. Mildness means the flavor doesn't compete with the dairy.

Hass avocado's buttery, grassy quality translates beautifully into a gelato base. The sea salt is not optional — it performs the same function as salt in caramel, drawing out the avocado's flavor compounds and providing contrast.

## Certified Organic Commitment

Naia's certification extends to every ingredient: the avocados (from a Central California farm), the dairy, the cane sugar, the salt. This is meaningfully harder to achieve than "uses some organic ingredients" — it requires supply chain management that most gelaterias don't attempt.

## The Full Menu

Naia also makes an outstanding pistachio, a dark chocolate that rivals European gelaterie, and seasonal stone fruit flavors in summer. The shop is small and the seating is outdoor, which makes warm Silicon Valley evenings the ideal visiting time.
