## The Machine That Changed Everything

Founder Robyn Sue Fisher built Robyn — the liquid nitrogen ice cream machine on wheels — in her Stanford engineering lab. When she opened Smitten's first location in Hayes Valley in 2011 inside a 60-square-foot former parking space, the city took notice immediately.

The technology means zero stabilizers, zero preservatives, and ice cream that is molecularly smaller than anything you've had before. The fat crystals are tiny. The texture is impossibly smooth.

## The Brown Butter Peach

The seasonal brown butter peach captures Brentwood peaches — grown in the East Bay — at peak ripeness in July and August. The brown butter adds a nutty warmth that makes the fruit sing. This flavor exists for maybe six weeks a year. When it's gone, it's gone.

## Year-Round Favorites

The hot fudge sundae (made with Guittard chocolate) and the sea salt caramel are perennial favorites when seasonal specials aren't running. Smitten's vanilla is also worth benchmarking — it shows what fresh liquid nitrogen ice cream tastes like against anything from a carton.

## Multiple Locations

Hayes Valley, the Ferry Building, and Palo Alto give you options across the Bay. The Ferry Building outpost has the best view.
