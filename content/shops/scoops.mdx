## East Hollywood's Underground Ice Cream Legend

Tai Kim opened Scoops in 2004 in a small storefront in East Hollywood and immediately started making ice cream that nobody else was making in LA: avocado jalapeño, bourbon nectarine, brown bread, Thai iced tea. The menu is written on a whiteboard and changes every single day based on what Tai feels like making.

## The Brown Bread Flavor

Brown bread ice cream is an Irish and British pub dessert tradition — stale brown bread toasted with butter and sugar, then folded into vanilla cream. Tai's version uses a dark, slightly sweet base (not quite chocolate, not quite caramel) with breadcrumbs that add a savory, nutty crunch.

It's deeply unfashionable-sounding and immediately addictive.

## The Anti-Instagram Aesthetic

Scoops has a handwritten sign, no website worth speaking of, and relies entirely on word of mouth. It has been written about in the New York Times, Bon Appétit, and the LA Times, and none of that has changed the shop's personality. You show up, you read the whiteboard, you pick something weird, and you are usually surprised by how good it is.

## The Neighborhood

East Hollywood / Los Feliz border — eclectic neighborhood with independent shops, good Thai food, and a DIY arts scene. Scoops fits perfectly. Bring cash to be safe.
