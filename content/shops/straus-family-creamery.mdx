## The Dairy Behind the Ice Cream

Albert Straus founded his creamery in Marshall in 1994 and became the first certified organic dairy west of the Mississippi. His farm sits on Tomales Bay in Point Reyes National Seashore — one of the most beautiful agricultural settings in California.

The Straus name appears in the ingredient list of countless Northern California food businesses. When Bi-Rite says "made with Straus cream," this is the farm they mean.

## The Soft Serve Experience

The on-farm soft serve stand is a few miles off Highway 1. You drive through working dairy country, smell hay and salt air, see the Tomales Bay through cypress trees, and get served the simplest soft serve imaginable: whole milk, organic cane sugar, nothing else.

It tastes like every artisan ice cream you've ever had, except cleaner. The dairy is so good that nothing else is needed.

## The Drive Out

Point Reyes is worth a full day trip from San Francisco: the lighthouse, the elk herds, the oyster farms on Tomales Bay, a lunch of fresh oysters at the oyster shacks, and then the Straus soft serve on the drive back. This is one of the great day trips from the Bay Area.

## The Organic Certification Story

Albert Straus's decision to go organic in the 1990s, before it was economically incentivized, was a philosophical choice. His rationale — that healthy soil produces healthier animals that produce better milk — is now accepted wisdom in the sustainable agriculture community.
