## 90 Years of San Diego Scooping

Hammond's has been in San Diego since 1935, making it a rare multi-generational ice cream tradition in Southern California. The original recipes have been preserved while the flavor menu expanded to include modern additions alongside the classics.

## The PB&J Execution

What separates Hammond's PB&J from a novelty flavor is the seriousness of the execution. The peanut butter is ground fresh and integrated into the ice cream base — not added as a swirl or mix-in but as the foundational flavor. The Concord grape jam is ribboned through in thick veins that create jammy pockets in every few bites.

The ratio matters. Too much jam and it becomes cloyingly sweet. Too little and it's just peanut butter ice cream. Hammond's has this calibrated correctly.

## San Diego Ice Cream Culture

San Diego gets less ice cream press than San Francisco and Los Angeles but has a serious creamery culture of its own. The weather helps — 70°F and sunny year-round means ice cream is always seasonally appropriate.

## Multiple Locations

Hammond's has grown to several San Diego locations including Mission Hills, Ocean Beach, and La Mesa. The Mission Hills location is the flagship and has the longest history.
