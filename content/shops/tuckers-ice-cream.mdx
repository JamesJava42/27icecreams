## Alameda's Eighty-Year Constant

Alameda is an island in the San Francisco Bay connected to Oakland by a series of tubes and bridges. It has a distinctly small-town feel despite being minutes from the Bay Area's biggest cities. Tucker's fits this — a shop that has been there longer than most residents have been alive, serving the same reliable flavors, staffed by teenagers who grew up eating there.

## The Cheesecake Base

Cheesecake ice cream requires getting the cream cheese tang into a frozen medium without the flavor curdling or becoming too dominant. Tucker's achieves the balance — there is genuine cream cheese sharpness in the base that distinguishes it from plain vanilla, but it's smooth enough to serve as backdrop for the marionberry.

## The Marionberry

Marionberry is a blackberry variety developed by the USDA in the 1940s, named for Marion County, Oregon. It's larger, more complex-flavored, and less tart than standard blackberries — earthy, slightly floral, with a deep jam quality when cooked down. Tucker's uses Oregon-sourced marionberry jam in thick ribbons.

## The Island Aesthetic

Walking to Tucker's on Park Street in the late afternoon, in this flat, leafy, semi-suburban California island, is a specific kind of Bay Area pleasure that guidebooks consistently miss.
