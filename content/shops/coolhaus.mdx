## The Architecture Pun That Built an Empire

Every Coolhaus sandwich is named after an architect or building: Frank Behry (Frank Gehry), Mintimalism, Frank Lloyd Bite. The concept was clever enough to get press immediately and functional enough to survive the novelty — the ice cream sandwiches are genuinely excellent.

## The Dirty Mint Chip

"Dirty" mint refers to using fresh mint steeped in cream, which produces a more complex, vegetal, slightly bitter flavor than peppermint extract. Real mint ice cream is grassy-green, not seafoam-green. It tastes like eating ice cream next to a mint plant rather than chewing mint gum.

The dark chocolate chunks are 72% — bitter enough to provide real contrast against the mint's freshness.

## The Cookie-to-Ice-Cream Ratio

The structural innovation of Coolhaus sandwiches is the cookie thickness. The cookies are thicker than standard — more like small cakes — which means they compress against the ice cream instead of shattering. The resulting bite has proper cookie structure, not the crumble mess of a standard ice cream sandwich.

## National Availability

Coolhaus pints are available at Whole Foods and other specialty grocery stores nationally. The sandwiches ship through Goldbelly. For readers outside California, this is the most accessible item on the entire Trail.
