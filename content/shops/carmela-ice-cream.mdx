## The San Gabriel Valley Ice Cream Scene

Pasadena and the greater San Gabriel Valley are Los Angeles County's hidden food destination — home to some of the best Chinese and Southeast Asian food in the country, plus a cluster of serious artisan food producers that the Westside food press consistently undercovers.

Carmela is the anchor of this scene. When Jessica Mortarotti opened the shop in 2008, she built her menu around the idea that LA deserved flavors as serious as its restaurant culture.

## Why Earl Grey

Earl Grey is deceptively complex. The bergamot — a citrus from Calabria, Italy — gives the tea a floral, slightly perfumed quality. When steeped into cream and churned cold, the bergamot becomes the star while the black tea tannins add structure. The shortbread provides sweetness and crunch without competing.

## The Farmers Market Connection

Carmela runs a stall at several San Gabriel Valley farmers markets, which is often where people encounter the brand for the first time. The shop itself has a neighborhood creamery feel — small, unhurried, with a long list of rotating specials written on a chalkboard.

## Other Must-Try Flavors

Salted butterscotch, cardamom, and the seasonal fresh fig are all worth trying. Carmela's horchata flavor — when available — is one of the better examples of that style outside of the Mexican ice cream specialists.
