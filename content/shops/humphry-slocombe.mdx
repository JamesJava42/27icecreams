## The Weirdest Breakfast in San Francisco

Jake Godby named his ice cream shop after a British sitcom character and built his menu around the idea that ice cream doesn't need to play it safe. Secret Breakfast — bourbon base, whole Corn Flakes folded in — was the first proof of concept.

The bourbon is present but not aggressive. The Corn Flakes soften in the ice cream but retain just enough crunch to provide texture. The result tastes like a very sophisticated adult cereal milk situation.

## The Philosophy

Humphry Slocombe's menu reads like a fever dream: Harvey Milk & Honey Graham, Foie Gras, Blue Bottle Vietnamese Coffee, Prosciutto. Not every flavor lands, but the mission — to destroy your preconceptions about what ice cream can be — is consistently achieved.

## Rotating Seasonal Specials

The regular menu rotates with seasons and whims. Past specials have included: Hibachi Lemon Curd, Thai Chili Lime, Beet Molasses. Check their Instagram before visiting — if something wild is on special, that's your opening to experiment.

## The Mission Neighborhood Context

Humphry Slocombe sits in the outer Mission, a neighborhood known for taquerias and warehouse galleries. Getting a Secret Breakfast cone and wandering through the neighborhood is one of the city's great unscripted afternoons.
