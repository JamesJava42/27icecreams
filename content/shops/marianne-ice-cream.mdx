## Three Quarters of a Century on Ocean Street

Marianne's opened in 1947 when Santa Cruz was still primarily an agricultural community and the boardwalk attracted summer tourists from the Central Valley. The shop survived the earthquake, the university's arrival, and the tech influx that transformed the Bay Area around it.

## The Burgundy Cherry Flavor

Burgundy cherry is an old-fashioned American ice cream parlor flavor that few shops make anymore. Dark Bing cherries are macerated in Burgundy wine, which deepens the fruit flavor and adds a faint vinous quality that pairs beautifully with vanilla cream. The cherries hold their structure and provide textural contrast throughout.

## The Scoop Size

Marianne's is famous for generous portions. A single scoop would pass for a double at most shops. A double scoop requires a bowl. This is part of the institution's DNA — you leave satisfied.

## The UCSC Connection

The University of California Santa Cruz opened in 1965 and generations of graduates have their Marianne's memories. Alumni who return for reunions often cite this shop specifically as a reason to return.
