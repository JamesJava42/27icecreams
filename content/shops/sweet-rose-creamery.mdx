## The Rustic Canyon Approach to Ice Cream

Josh Loeb and Zoe Nathan built a small restaurant empire on the Westside of Los Angeles — Rustic Canyon, Milo & Olive, Huckleberry — before opening Sweet Rose. The philosophy is identical across all of them: source impeccably, prep carefully, let the ingredients speak.

## The Caramelization Process

The caramelized banana isn't just blended banana in ice cream. The bananas are halved and cooked in butter and sugar until genuinely caramelized — dark brown, jammy, deeply flavored — before being folded into the cream base. The heat transforms the fruit chemistry, concentrating it and adding a roasted quality that raw bananas never have.

## The Brentwood Location

The original Sweet Rose is in the Brentwood Country Mart, a tucked-away outdoor shopping complex where Westsiders have been coming for decades. The atmosphere is relaxed, shaded, and distinctly neighborhood. Farmers market proximity means seasonal flavors change weekly.

## Farmers Market Sourcing

Sweet Rose participates in and sources from the Santa Monica Farmers Market, one of the best in the country. Summer stone fruits — peaches, nectarines, apricots — get the caramelization treatment in season, creating temporary flavors worth checking their Instagram for.
