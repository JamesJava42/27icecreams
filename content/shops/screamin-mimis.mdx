## Pioneer of the Sonoma Food Scene

Mimi opened her creamery on Sebastopol Avenue years before the farm-to-table movement made small-batch everything a buzzword. She was simply making ice cream with local dairy because that's what was available and what tasted best.

## The Wine Country Setting

Sebastopol sits at the edge of the Russian River Valley wine appellation, surrounded by apple orchards and vineyards. Screamin' Mimi's fits this context perfectly — a place that takes a simple pleasure seriously, using the best local ingredients, without any pretension.

## Seasonal Specials

The autumn menu features apple cider sorbet and fresh-pressed Gravenstein apple ice cream (Sebastopol is famous for its Gravenstein apples). Summer brings fresh peach and berry flavors from surrounding farms.

## A Genuine Road Trip Stop

Screamin' Mimi's is best enjoyed as part of a Sonoma road trip itinerary. Drive out from the city on a Saturday, hit a few wineries, stop at a farm stand, and finish the day with a cone here before the drive back. It's been that trip for generations of Bay Area families.
