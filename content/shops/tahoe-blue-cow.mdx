## Ice Cream at 6,300 Feet

There is something philosophically correct about eating ice cream at high altitude. The cold, dry Sierra Nevada air, the smell of pine, the view of Lake Tahoe through the trees — Tahoe Blue Cow leans into all of it.

## The Brown Butter Base

Brown butter ice cream isn't common because browning the butter without burning it requires precision. Blue Cow's kitchen has it dialed in. The result is a nutty, caramel-adjacent base that tastes richer and more complex than standard butter ice cream.

## Summer vs. Winter Visits

Summer visitors get the full patio experience with lake views and afternoon light. Winter visitors get hot chocolate also available — and the particular joy of eating ice cream in ski boots, sitting outside in 30°F weather because why not.

## The Cookie Dough

Made in-house without raw eggs (food-safe), the cookie dough chunks are properly sized — big enough to get real chocolate chip cookie flavor, small enough that every bite has some. The integration with the brown butter base is the key.
