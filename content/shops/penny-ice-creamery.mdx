## Santa Cruz's Neighborhood Creamery

Kendra Baker and Zachary Davis started Penny with a commitment to the 50-mile sourcing radius: nearly everything comes from within a short drive of the shop. Straus Family dairy from Marshall. Honey from local apiaries in the Santa Cruz Mountains. Fruit from Swanton Berry Farm.

## The Lavender Honey Flavor

The lavender is measured precisely — too much and it tastes like soap; too little and it disappears. Penny's version hits the ideal midpoint. The local honey brings sweetness with a faint wildflower note. The resulting ice cream tastes like the Pacific coast smells on a warm morning.

## The Menu Approach

Penny changes their menu regularly based on what's available locally. The guiding principle: make flavors that couldn't be made anywhere else using ingredients that don't travel well. A summer strawberry scoop made with fruit picked that morning is categorically different from frozen strawberries shipped across the country.

## Two Locations

The Cedar Street location is the original and has a neighborhood ice cream parlor feel. The Westside shop is a bit larger. Both are within walking distance of downtown Santa Cruz's beach boardwalk area.
